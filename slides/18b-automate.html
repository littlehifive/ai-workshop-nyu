Part 2 · The Tool Landscape

A fourth capability is emerging: AI that automates work on your behalf

Beyond asking, doing, and remembering, the newest systems learn enough about your work to carry out recurring tasks on their own, on a schedule or in response to events, under supervision rules that you define. You set the standing instructions once; the system runs and reports back.

For a researcher

A weekly scan of new papers in your subfield, summarized and delivered every Monday morning.

For a team

Meeting recordings transcribed, summarized, and filed to the right project folder without anyone asking.

For a study

A data-quality check that runs after each survey wave and flags exceptions for human review.

Outside today's scope We will not cover automation today. The principle you will practice applies here with greater force: the more a system acts for you, the more deliberately you must verify its work.
