Part 2 · The Tool Landscape

Our recommendation: choose by task, then anchor on one primary tool

Analyzing long documents and writing with care → Claude Strong sustained reasoning over book-length material. Projects hold your sources and standing instructions.
Question-answering over a fixed set of sources → NotebookLM A closed system by design. Every answer carries citations that link back to a passage you can check.
Your documents already live in Google Workspace → Gemini Gems stay synced with Drive, so the assistant reads the current version of every file.
Your institution runs on Microsoft 365 → Copilot Works inside Word, Excel, Outlook, and Teams, governed by the permissions your organization already set.
You want the largest community and guides → ChatGPT The most widely used assistant, with the deepest pool of tutorials, templates, and shared custom assistants.
Commit to one primary tool for at least a quarter and learn its project features well. When you do re-evaluate, test it on your own tasks with your own questions rather than relying on launch-day announcements.
